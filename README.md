# Lost in Translation:
What Do Duolingo Users Actually Want?
An analysis of 80 online Duolingo reviews sourced from Reddit and Google Play.

## Overview
This purpose of this project was to understand what users actually want from a language learning app, how satisfied they are, and whether the app is meeting their underlying goals. Reviews were manually collected, categorized using Claude and ChatGPT, and visualized in Power BI.

## Key Questions
• What types of users are actually using Duolingo, and what do they want from it?
• How satisfied are users and does satisfaction vary by user type?
• What unmet needs do users most commonly express?
• How many users are travel-motivated, and does that shape their experience?
• What do users do when Duolingo doesn't meet their needs? Do they stay, add resources, or quit?

## Toolset
Claude & ChatGPT (review summarization, categorization, and column coding)*
Excel & Power Query (data cleaning & transformation)
Power BI (data visualization)
Affinity Suite (presentation deck design)

*A note on AI's role: Claude * ChatGPT were used to read each review and assign values across structured columns (user type, satisfaction, unmet need, travel motivation, etc.). Categorizations were reviewed and confirmed manually before being used in the final analysis.

## User Type Definitions
• Casual Learner - learning a language but not necessarily to fluency; low pressure, flexible goals
• Fluency Seeker - aims to learn to the point of fluency
• Supplementer - uses Duolingo alongside other methods
• Brain Trainer - seniors and casual users keeping mentally sharp
• Streak Keeper - motivated by the gamification loop more than language acquisition

## Key Insights
• Casual Learners and Fluency Seekers are the largest user subsets
• Fluency Seekers express the highest levels of dissatisfaction
• Grammar and fewer session limitations are the most-cited unmet needs 

Data Sources: Reddit (r/duolingo) and Google Play Store
